## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Professional but never stuffy. Confident without bragging. Self-deprecating humor used sparingly and always punching at the work, never the person.

**Style:** Direct and concrete. Short taglines followed by a single sentence of grounding context. First-person, no royal "we." Emojis used functionally (one per item, never decorative clusters).

**Personality:** Pragmatic, understated, quietly witty.

**Opener archetype:** Warm greeting (`Hello 👋`) immediately followed by a single concrete capability claim — no fluff before the value.

### Canonical voice examples

**Good — opener:**

> "Hello 👋, I'm Josemy Duarte. I assist companies in creating scalable and reliable cloud applications, ensuring service stability through technical excellence."

**Good — project tagline (understated humor):**

> "Find out exactly how many days of your life you've donated to the screen. Because someone had to put a number on your TV addiction." _(TraktWastedTime)_

**Good — projects section sub-headline:**

> "Building tools that solve real problems and make life easier through code and creativity."

### We say / We don't say

| ✅ We say                                    | ❌ We don't say                                  |
| -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| "scalable and reliable"                      | "world-class solutions"                          |
| "ensuring service stability"                 | "passionate about technology"                    |
| "Building tools that solve real problems"    | "I love coding"                                  |
| Self-deprecating humor about the _work_      | Hyperbole about skills                           |
| Concrete tech names (Go, Cloudflare Workers) | "cutting-edge", "innovative", "state-of-the-art" |
| One emoji per card/item as a visual anchor   | Emoji clusters for decoration                    |

### Anti-patterns to avoid

- "Passionate developer" / "I love coding"
- Vague buzzwords: "innovative," "cutting-edge," "world-class," "synergy"
- Walls of text without visual breaks
- Listing every framework ever touched (curation beats completeness)
- Apologetic hedging ("just a small project," "it's nothing fancy")
